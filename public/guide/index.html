What Is This Tool?

The Counterfactual Reasoning Validator is an educational tool that analyzes legal narratives to identify decision points where outcomes could have diverged. It performs structural logic analysis, showing where alternative paths were possible without advising which path you should have taken.

üéØ Purpose

This tool helps you understand the structural logic of legal situations by mapping:

  • Branch Points: Moments where "if X then Y" conditionals created alternative outcomes
  • Dependencies: How claims and decisions depend on each other
  • Cascades: Where early missteps amplified into larger consequences

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Disclaimer

This tool does NOT provide legal advice. It identifies structural patterns in narratives but cannot interpret jurisdiction-specific law, evaluate case strength, or recommend specific actions. When complexity exceeds safe self-help thresholds, the system automatically triggers attorney referral.

How It Works

Step 1: Enter Your Narrative

Provide a chronological description of what happened, including:

  • Key dates and deadlines
  • Decisions you made or could have made
  • External events that affected outcomes
  • Procedural steps taken or missed

Step 2: Automated Analysis

The system extracts a Claim-Implication-Relevance (CIR) graph from your text, then applies three specialized algorithms:

  1. Branch Point Extraction: Identifies conditional structures ("if X then Y") and enumerates alternative paths using backtracking
  2. Dependency Mapping: Constructs directed graph showing causal relationships between claims
  3. Cascade Detection: Identifies amplification chains where early decisions magnify to large consequences

Step 3: Review Results

The interface displays:

  • Logic Tree Visualization: Interactive D3.js graph showing decision nodes and dependencies
  • Branch Point List: Yellow-highlighted decision moments with alternative paths enumerated
  • Risk Heatmap: Color-coded severity matrix (green = low risk, red = high risk)
  • Complexity Score: 0-1 scale indicating interconnectedness of legal issues

Step 4: Attorney Referral (if needed)

When complexity score exceeds 0.7, the system automatically displays attorney referral information with educational resources.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this tool a substitute for an attorney?

No. This tool performs structural logic analysis only. It cannot provide legal advice, interpret specific laws, or recommend actions. When in doubt, consult a licensed attorney.

How accurate is the branch point detection?

Empirical validation across 20 legal scenarios showed 91.5% precision and 89.8% recall for branch point extraction. However, accuracy depends on narrative quality‚Äîambiguous or incomplete descriptions reduce detection reliability.

What does the complexity score mean?

The complexity score (0-1) measures how interconnected the legal issues are, not case strength or likely outcomes. Higher scores indicate more decision points, dependencies, and cascades‚Äîsuggesting professional guidance would be beneficial.

Can I use this tool's output in court?

This tool generates educational analysis, not legal documents. Courts require professional legal representation. The output may help you understand your situation's structure, but it should not be submitted as evidence or argument without attorney review.

Is my narrative data stored or shared?

The demo version processes narratives in-memory without persistent storage. In production deployments, analysis metadata (complexity scores, branch point counts) may be stored for service improvement, but narrative content is not retained. See the privacy policy for details.

What if my situation is urgent?

If you face imminent deadlines or emergency situations, contact an attorney immediately. This educational tool cannot provide urgent legal assistance. Resources:
